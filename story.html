Our story

Founded by a journalist who tells a place by its people.

← Back home

Hannah Berkeley Cohen is an Ohio native who has called the Caribbean home since 2012. After a semester abroad at the University of Havana, she moved to Cuba to document its youth culture, everyday middle-class life, and dwindling Jewish community. By 2015 she had become the New York Times' primary stringer in Havana, reporting on American foreign policy toward Cuba, the death of Fidel Castro, and the historic visits of President Obama and the Pope.

In Havana she met dozens of Jewish groups — organised by the JDC, by synagogues across North America, and by individuals returning to Cuba for the first time since their parents or grandparents left half a century earlier. She founded Cuba Rising, a bespoke travel company for Americans seeking the complex realities of modern Cuba, with group sizes capped at eight for flexibility and depth.

Using that experience, Hannah moved to Curaçao in 2024 to delve deeper into the island's Jewish history and heritage, and to create culturally-relevant programmes for North American travellers. She is fluent in English and Spanish, and is enjoying learning Dutch and Papiamentu.

What to expect: immersive journeys of people-to-people encounters — time with prominent members of the local Jewish community, storytelling at historic sites, and dialogues with scholars, artists, and cultural trailblazers.
